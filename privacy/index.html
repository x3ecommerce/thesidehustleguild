Section 02

What we collect

In plain English

Three buckets: what you give us (email, name, payment via Whop/Stripe), what Discord shares (your username and role when you join the server), and what we measure (anonymous page views, which buttons you click, technical info like browser type). We do not collect government IDs, biometric data, or precise GPS location.

2.1 Information you provide.

  • Email address (for newsletters, account confirmation, support)
  • Display name and Discord username
  • Payment information — processed by Whop and Stripe; we receive transaction metadata (date, amount, last 4 of card) but not full card numbers
  • Hustle Card submissions, forum posts, messages you send in our Discord, contest entries
  • Tax info (W-9, address) only if you receive prize payouts over $600/year

2.2 Information automatically collected.

  • Anonymous analytics events (pages viewed, buttons clicked) — stored in Cloudflare and our D1 database
  • Technical data — IP address (truncated for analytics), browser user-agent, device type
  • Server-side logs of API requests for security and debugging

2.3 Information from third parties.

  • From Discord: your Discord ID, username, server-role when you join the Guild
  • From Whop: subscription status, renewal date, affiliate referral code if applicable
  • From Stripe: payment confirmation, refund status
Section 03

How we use it

In plain English

To run the Guild — verify your membership, send you the newsletter, process prize payouts, keep things working. We do not sell your data to advertisers. We do not train AI models on your private messages.

We process your personal data for the following purposes:

  • Service delivery: grant Discord access, verify payment status, deliver contest payouts, send transactional emails
  • Communication: respond to your support requests, send weekly newsletter (you can unsubscribe anytime)
  • Improvement: understand which content resonates, measure conversion, fix broken pages
  • Legal: comply with tax reporting (1099 for prize winners over $600), respond to lawful requests, defend our legal interests
  • Security: detect fraud, block bots, prevent abuse of the platform

Our legal bases under GDPR are: contract (delivering the service you paid for), legitimate interest (analytics, security), and consent (marketing emails, optional cookies).

Section 04

Who we share with

In plain English

Only the vendors that help us run the Guild — none of them get your data to use for their own marketing. They process it only to provide their service to us.

We share personal data with the following categories of service providers ("data processors"), each bound by data processing agreements:

  • Payment & subscription: Whop (merchant of record), Stripe (payments)
  • Hosting & infrastructure: Cloudflare (Pages, Workers, D1, R2, Email Routing)
  • Email: Resend (transactional + newsletter)
  • Community platform: Discord
  • Forms: Tally
  • AI processing: Anthropic (Claude API, used for content moderation and concierge messages — your private DMs are not sent)
  • Analytics: Our own first-party D1 database (no third-party analytics tool sells your data)
  • Tax / accounting: Our accountant and the IRS (only for payouts ≥ $600/year)

We will disclose data to law enforcement only when legally required (e.g., valid subpoena). We will notify you unless prohibited by law.

We do not sell your personal information. We do not share it with advertisers, data brokers, or AI training datasets.

Section 05

Cookies and tracking

In plain English

We use a small number of cookies. The strictly-necessary ones are always on (they're what makes the site work). Everything else is optional — you control it in the cookie banner at the bottom of the page, or by visiting our cookies preferences page.

5.1 Strictly necessary (always on). Session cookies, CSRF tokens, login state. Cannot be disabled without breaking the site.

5.2 Analytics (optional). A first-party cookie that records anonymous page visits to our own database. No third-party analytics provider.

5.3 Marketing (optional). Used only if we run a paid ad campaign — to measure conversion. Off by default.

You can change your preferences anytime at /cookies/. EU and UK visitors will see a consent banner on first visit.

Section 06

Your rights (GDPR, CCPA)

In plain English

You can ask us to show you, correct, or delete your data. Email privacy@thesidehustleguild.com and we respond within 30 days. California residents have additional rights under CCPA.

Under GDPR (EU/UK) and CCPA (California), you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you
  • Correct inaccurate data
  • Delete your data ("right to erasure")
  • Export your data in a portable format
  • Object to specific processing (e.g., marketing)
  • Withdraw consent at any time without affecting prior lawful processing
  • Lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority

To exercise any of these rights, email privacy@thesidehustleguild.com. We will verify your identity and respond within 30 days (or 45 days for complex requests, with notice).

California residents: you have the right to opt out of "sale" of personal information. We do not sell personal information, so there is no opt-out to exercise — but you may still confirm this in writing.

Section 07

Data retention

In plain English

We keep your data while you're a member, plus a reasonable period after — for tax records, fraud prevention, and in case you want to come back. You can request deletion anytime.

Default retention periods:

  • Account data: while active + 24 months after cancellation
  • Payment records: 7 years (IRS requirement)
  • Tax forms (W-9, 1099): 7 years (IRS requirement)
  • Analytics events: 12 months in raw form, then aggregated indefinitely
  • Discord messages: retained by Discord per their policies; the Guild does not maintain an independent archive of member chat
  • Email logs: 6 months
Section 08

How we protect your data

In plain English

TLS everywhere, encrypted databases, short-lived access tokens, and strict permissions on who can read what. If we ever have a breach affecting you, we tell you within 72 hours.

Technical and organizational measures include: TLS 1.3 for all traffic, encrypted at-rest storage (Cloudflare D1, R2), restricted access via principle of least privilege, regular rotation of API tokens, no plaintext storage of credentials, audit logs of administrative actions.

In the event of a personal data breach likely to result in a high risk to your rights, we will notify you and the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware, per GDPR Art. 33–34.
