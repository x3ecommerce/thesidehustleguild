The honest part: the "must remain paid" gate

Both sides of every commission have to be active paid members. Always.

If a referral cancels their subscription, that referral's commission stops accruing. If they reactivate later, it resumes from that point. Refunds and chargebacks reverse the corresponding commission within the 30-day window.

If you cancel your own membership, your entire commission stream pauses until you reactivate. We don't pay people to quit the room they're recommending.

This gate is what keeps the program clean. It's also what protects you — every dollar the Guild owes you comes from a real subscription that's still being paid. No mystery accounting.

Anti-gaming protections

We protect the program so the people doing it the right way actually get paid. Six guardrails, plain English.

30-day ref qualification

A referral counts toward your milestones only after they've stayed paid for 30 consecutive days. Day-one signups don't trigger a bonus.

Vested bonuses

Cash milestones pay in installments — $50/mo, $200/quarter, $500/half-year — only while the cohort stays active. Worst-case same-week exposure is $800, not $4,350.

10% MRR ceiling

Total monthly cash bonuses across all affiliates are capped at 10% of that month's MRR. If we'd blow the cap, payouts prorate and defer. The math always pencils.

Refund-protected commissions

Commissions don't accrue until the referral's payment clears the 30-day chargeback window. A reversed payment reverses the commission automatically.

Velocity flag

Ten or more referrals from one affiliate in 48 hours triggers a manual review. Not a freeze — we just verify they're not duplicate or coerced signups.

Burden of proof on us

If we deny a milestone for suspected gaming, we have to show evidence. You're not presumed guilty. Appeals are reviewed by the Tribe Council.

FAQ

Do I have to be a paying member to be an affiliate?
No — but you have to remain active in the program. You can apply as a non-member affiliate and earn from the standard tier. Members get one advantage: their referrals automatically count toward their own milestone progression.
When do I get paid?
Monthly, on the 1st, for the prior month's earnings, after the 30-day chargeback window clears. Minimum payout threshold is $50 — under that, the balance rolls forward. Payouts go via Stripe Connect or Wise (your choice at signup).
Is this an MLM? Do I earn off other affiliates' referrals?
No. The Guild is and will remain a single-tier program. You earn only on the people you personally referred. There's no downline, no matrix, no "build your team and earn from their team." That's a deliberate legal and ethical choice.
What happens to my commissions if I lose a referral?
That referral's commission stops the day they cancel. Your active count drops by one, which may affect your tier going forward. Past commissions already paid are yours. Past milestone bonuses already vested are yours. Future installments of vested bonuses pause if your active count drops below the threshold.
What if my referral upgrades to Operator or Founders Circle later?
Your commission rate applies to whatever they pay. If your referral upgrades from Lab Member ($19/mo) to Operator ($99/mo), your monthly commission scales accordingly.
Can I lose my role if I drop below the threshold?
Verified Affiliate, Tribe Builder, and Tribe Council roles are conditional — they revert if you stay below threshold for 90+ days. The Guild Masthead listing and badge are permanent once earned, even if your active count later drops. The 40% rate, however, is contingent on staying above 100 active refs.
What about taxes?
If you earn $600+ in a calendar year as a US affiliate, the IRS requires 1099 reporting. Stripe Connect (which handles your payouts) auto-issues a 1099-K when the threshold is hit — we don't issue it ourselves. Non-US affiliates submit a W-8BEN at signup and may have withholding applied.
What gets me removed from the program?
Spam, cold DMs to strangers, sock-puppet referrals, self-referrals, MLM-style downline language, false urgency tactics, hidden affiliate links, or misuse of the Guild brand. Removal forfeits all unvested milestone installments. We document every removal with evidence — no gut-call freezes.
