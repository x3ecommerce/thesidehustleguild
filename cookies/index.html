Cookie preferences

You decide which optional cookies to allow. Strictly necessary cookies are always on — they're what makes login and the site itself work. Everything else is up to you.

Strictly necessary

Required for the site to function. Cannot be disabled. Includes session cookies for staying logged in, CSRF tokens for security, and your cookie-preference setting itself.

Examples: shg_session, shg_csrf, shg_cookie_prefs

Analytics

First-party analytics so we can see which pages people visit and which buttons get clicked. No third-party analytics provider — the data stays in our own database. Used only in aggregate.

Examples: shg_analytics (first-party, anonymous page-view counter)

Marketing

Used only if we run a paid advertising campaign — to measure conversion from an ad. Off by default. No data shared with ad platforms unless you enable this.

Examples: shg_utm_attribution (only set when arriving from a paid ad)

Personalization

Used to remember your tier/role preferences across sessions so the Discord onboarding doesn't ask the same questions twice. Anonymous, never shared.

Examples: shg_role_pref, shg_tier_pref
